About Me

My name is Jeff Ford and I was born on April 12th of 1980, in Aberdeen, Washington. The youngest child in my family, I grew up in Aberdeen and attended the local public high school, where I was a left-handed pitcher on the baseball team.

After high school, I moved to Seattle to attend the University of Washington. I eventually graduated from UW with a Bachelor's degree in political science.

I began working in the state legislature after college, and soon joined the Army Reserves. The decision to join the Army Reserves was a big one, and it had a more significant impact on my life than I expected. In my thirteen years of service, I was on active-duty for five of them.

One benefit of the military was that I had the opportunity to see new places. Places that I have lived because of the military include:

  • Texas
  • South Carolina
  • Virginia

I also lived in Washington, DC (unrelated to the military), and did three overseas deployments.
